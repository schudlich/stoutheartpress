# **About Stout Heart Press**

![Stout Heart Press Logo](printersdevice.png)

### Tanner Schudlich (he/they) is a social worker, archivist, printmaker, and book binder based in Ypsilanti, MI. Tanner’s work engages with questions about how we navigate grief, memory, hope, and despair in our lives and communities. Tanner uses his own Vandercook 099 cylinder proofing press in addition to community accessible Vandercook No. 3 and No. 1 presses at the University of Michigan Book Arts Studio. When not teaching or working in the U-M Book Arts Studio, he runs Stout Heart Press, a small private press, out of his apartment where he lives with his partner and two cats.

## Timeline

### 2023
At some point during the Summer of 2023 I bought a pack of pink pearl erasers to play with a vintage linocut tool from the thrift store. With some watercolor paper and a black stamp pad, I began making cards with repeating patterns.

I made my first press - like a giant tortilla press, made with two Ikea cutting boards and a few hinges. It worked surprisingly well for a few linocuts, but eventually the lever I made wore out.

### 2024
I began my Library Science graduate program at the University of Michigan in the Fall of 2024, at which point I discovered the Book Arts Studio on campus. I began working with moveable type and large proofing presses, learning from Ann Arbor printer and wood engraver, Jim Horton. 

### 2025
By the middle of my graduate program, book binding, letterpress, and linocut printing became ways of taking care of myself. I loved working with printmaking-centered collections in the archives I worked in on campus.

### 2026
Over the past year I have spent as much time as possible working in the studio, have taught several letterpress classes and tutored individual students working on book binding projects.

In April, I purchased a Vandercook 099 from Paul Moxon and began collecting type and tools to build my personal collection.
